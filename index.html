What’s Happening in Eritrea

Historical Conflicts

Eritrean War of Independence (1961–1991): Eritrea fought a 30-year war against Ethiopia, leading to its independence in 1993.

First Eritrean Civil War (1972–1974): Internal conflicts arose between the Eritrean Liberation Front (ELF) and emerging factions, resulting in approximately 3,000 casualties. [Source: Wikipedia]

Second Eritrean Civil War (1980–1981): The Eritrean People's Liberation Front (EPLF) clashed with the ELF, ultimately pushing the ELF into Sudan. [Source: Wikipedia]

Eritrean-Ethiopian War (1998–2000): A border dispute escalated into a full-scale war, resulting in significant casualties and displacement. [Source: Wikipedia]

Recent Developments

Suspension of Flights: In September 2024, Ethiopian Airlines halted flights to Eritrea, citing "very difficult operating conditions," indicating strained relations between the two nations. [Source: Reuters]

Human Rights Concerns: Reports from January 2025 highlight severe human rights abuses within Eritrea's secret prisons, including torture and inhumane conditions. [Source: The Guardian]

International Perception: Eritrea is often referred to as the "North Korea of Africa" due to its isolationist policies and human rights violations. [Source: HuffPost Español]

Cultural Reflection

Literary Insight: The novel "Oromay" by Baalu Girma, recently translated into English, offers a fictionalized account of Ethiopia's failed campaign to suppress Eritrean separatists in the 1980s, providing cultural context to the region's history. [Source: Financial Times]
